Evolution of the 3 Point Shot

One fundamental change in the past 30 years is the evolution of the 3 point shot. Introduced in 1979-80 season, the NBA adopted a three point line 23 feet and 9 inches away from the basket. Adoption of the 3-point shot was slow in the first few years. With teams favoring 2-point shots due to the perception of 2-point shots being a "higher percentage" shot.


Since then however, the 3-pointer has become an integral part of the game. The trend can be reflected in the amount of three pointers taken league wide. In 1990, the league as a whole took a total of 15,361 three point shots. In 2017, the league took a total of 73,136 three point shots, a nearly 400% increase. A number of other interesting facts are played out in the statistics as well. During the 1994-95, 1995-96, and 1996-97 seasons, the NBA shortened the distance of the 3 point line from 23ft 9 inches to a uniform 22 ft around the basket. This led to a increase in the amount of three point shots taken as well. In the 1997-98, season, the three point line was changed back, and the results can be seen in the sharp drop of three pointers from the 96-97 season to the 97-98 season. Since then, there has been a steady increase in the number of three point shots taken has increased. The most noticable increase can be seen in the modern era. Specifically, the league took 38,277 three pointers in 2012, yet this amount has since doubled to 73,136 in 2017. Driven by an increase in analytics backing the efficiency of the 3-point shot. Intuitively, shooting 33% on 3-point shots is identical to shooting 50% on 2-point shots. Thus, a nunmber of teams in the modern era have led the charge in shooting three pointers.

The teams that have been the quickest at adopting the 3-pointer have been the Houston Rockets, Golden State Warriors and the Cleveland Cavaliers. In particular, the Houston Rockets shot a record 3,306 3-pointers in the 2017 season, well higher than any other team in NBA history. This amount driven by analytics savvy GM Daryl Morey, and the acquisition of James Harden in 2012. The Rockets shot just 1,330 3-pointers in 2012.

Another driving force behind the rise of the 3-pointer is the increased effectiveness of shooters. The league as a whole shot an average of 22% on three pointers in 1990. In 2017, they shot an average of 30% on three pointers on 400% greater shooting volume. If players are becoming better shooters, what reason is there to not shoot three pointers?


The above chart shows the number of 2 point attempts versus the number of three point attempts by the top (in terms of three point shots made) three point shooters from 2000 to 2018. We can see that initially, the points are concentrated on the lower and lower right regions of the scatter plot, indicating higher volumes of 2-point shots and lower volumes of 3-point shots. However, in later years, the points start to move towards the upper region of the scatter plot, indicating higher and higher three point attempts. Some of the best shooters are now attempting more three point shots than two point shots. One name also stands out above the rest in this plot, as the two points near the top of the plot: Stephen Curry, having attempted nearly 2000 three pointers between 2016 and 2017. Looking at the trends, it is clear the affinity for the 3-point shot is not only growing but will continue to grow.

Impact of Defensive Rule Changes

The following graph shows nine NBA teams defense statistics from 1985 to 2016. The three graphs is allowed 3pt attempt, allowed assist and allowed free throw attempt. The major concept of this visualizations is to observe the impact of rule changes in NBA to the games. There are three major changes of the rules that affects the statistics of games directly. The first one is shortened the three-point line (22 feet in the corners extending to 23 feet, nine inches at the top of the key) to a uniform 22 feet around the basket in 1993. This as illustrated before, gave a huge boost to the three point made. The second one is the three-point line lengthened to its original distance of 23 feet, nine inches, except in the corners, where the distance remained 22 feet in 1996. This lead to the decrease of the three point, and as many of the three point made comes with an assist, the assist amount also decreased in two seasons. And then coaches developed different strategy to get used to the rules, which lead to the increase after two seasons of adjustment. In 1997 defender will not be permitted to use his forearm to impede the progress of an offensive player who is facing the basket in the front court. This rule so called the hand check rule lead to more fouls in the next season, which lead to more free throw attempt made.
